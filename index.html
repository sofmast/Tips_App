The Importance of Medical Oxygen

Medical oxygen is an essential life-saving treatment used in emergency medicine, intensive care, surgery, and respiratory therapy.

Proper oxygen therapy helps maintain healthy organ function, improves breathing efficiency, and supports patient recovery.

Important: Oxygen should always be prescribed and monitored by qualified medical professionals to avoid complications and ensure patient safety.

Conditions That May Require Oxygen Therapy

  • Severe Anemia
  • Carbon Monoxide Poisoning
  • Shock (All Types)
  • Prematurity (Underdeveloped Lungs)
  • Neonatal Respiratory Distress Syndrome
  • Pulmonary Hypertension of the Newborn
  • COPD During Exacerbations
  • Severe Asthma Attacks
  • Pneumonia
  • Tuberculosis (Advanced Cases)
  • Acute Respiratory Distress Syndrome
  • Pulmonary Edema
  • Heart Failure
  • Acute Myocardial Infarction (If Hypoxic)
  • Congenital Heart Disease
